What is Grass?

Grass is the common name for the plant family Poaceae, found on every continent except Antarctica. It covers about 20% of Earth's land surface and is essential to life as we know it.

Why Grass Matters

Erosion Control

Grass roots form dense networks that hold soil in place, preventing erosion and maintaining healthy landscapes.

Food Source

Grasses like wheat, rice, corn, and barley provide staple foods for billions of people and animals worldwide.

Oxygen Production

Like all plants, grass produces oxygen through photosynthesis, contributing to the air we breathe.

Natural Cooling

Lawns and grasslands help cool the environment, reducing urban heat island effects in cities.

Common Types of Grass

  • Bermuda Grass: Drought-resistant, popular for lawns and sports fields
  • Kentucky Bluegrass: Cool-season grass with rich green color
  • Zoysia Grass: Dense, carpet-like growth pattern
  • Fescue: Shade-tolerant, good for cooler climates
  • Bamboo: Fast-growing giant grass used for construction

Basic Lawn Care Tips

Watering

Water deeply but infrequently to encourage deep root growth. Early morning is best.

Mowing

Never cut more than 1/3 of the blade height at once. Keep mower blades sharp.

Fertilizing

Apply fertilizer in spring and fall based on your grass type and climate.
