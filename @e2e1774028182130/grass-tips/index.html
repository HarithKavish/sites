Top Lawn-Care Tips

Mow High

Keep grass blades 2.5-3.5 inches tall to shade roots and crowd out weeds.

Water Deeply, Not Often

One inch of water once a week encourages deep root growth.

Sharpen Mower Blades

Clean cuts reduce disease risk and stress on grass plants.

Leave Clippings

Grass cycling returns nitrogen to the soil-free fertilizer!

Aerate in Spring or Fall

Core aeration relieves compaction and boosts nutrient uptake.

Test Your Soil

A $15 soil test tells you exactly what nutrients your lawn needs.

Seasonal Calendar

SeasonKey Tasks
Spring Rake debris, apply pre-emergent, first mow (high)
Summer Water deeply, mow regularly, spot-treat weeds
Fall Fertilize, overseed, aerate, lower mower height
Winter Service equipment, plan next years seed/fertilizer

Essential Tools

  • Sharp mower with adjustable height
  • Rain gauge or small cups to measure irrigation
  • Core aerator (rental or manual)
  • Broadcast spreader for seed and fertilizer
  • Soil test kit
