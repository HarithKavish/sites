What is Grass?

Grass is one of the most important plant families on Earth, covering nearly 40% of land surfaces. From the lush lawns in your neighborhood to the vast savannas of Africa, grasses play a crucial role in our ecosystem.

Types of Grass

Cool-Season Grasses

Thrive in temperatures between 60-75°F. Perfect for northern climates.

  • Kentucky Bluegrass
  • Perennial Ryegrass
  • Tall Fescue

Warm-Season Grasses

Prefer temperatures between 80-95°F. Ideal for southern regions.

  • Bermuda Grass
  • Zoysia Grass
  • St. Augustine Grass

Why Grass Matters

🌱 Erosion Control

Grass roots hold soil in place, preventing erosion and maintaining healthy land.

🌬️ Air Purification

Grasses absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen, helping clean our air.

🏠 Wildlife Habitat

Provides food and shelter for countless insects, birds, and small animals.

💧 Water Management

Helps absorb rainfall and reduces runoff, protecting waterways.

Grass Care Tips

Watering

Water deeply but infrequently to encourage deep root growth.

Mowing

Never cut more than 1/3 of the grass blade length at once.

Fertilizing

Apply fertilizer in spring and fall for best results.

Aeration

Aerate compacted soil to improve water and nutrient absorption.
