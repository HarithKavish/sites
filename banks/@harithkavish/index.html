Understanding Banks

Your comprehensive guide to financial institutions, their functions, and services

Types of Banks

🏦

Commercial Banks

Traditional banks that accept deposits, provide checking and savings accounts, and offer loans to individuals and businesses.

💰

Investment Banks

Specialized institutions that help companies raise capital, facilitate mergers and acquisitions, and provide financial advisory services.

🌐

Central Banks

National institutions that manage a country's monetary policy, issue currency, and regulate the banking system.

👥

Credit Unions

Member-owned financial cooperatives that provide traditional banking services with a focus on community benefits.

🏠

Savings & Loan

Institutions that specialize in accepting savings deposits and making mortgage and other loans.

🌍

Online Banks

Digital-only institutions that operate without physical branches, often offering higher interest rates and lower fees.

History of Banking

2000 BCE

Ancient Banking

Early banking activities began in Mesopotamia with grain loans to farmers and traders.

1400s

Modern Banking Emerges

Italian banks like Medici Bank established modern banking practices including double-entry bookkeeping.

1694

Bank of England

The first central bank was established, creating the model for modern central banking.

1913

Federal Reserve System

The U.S. Federal Reserve was created to provide central banking functions for the United States.

1930s

FDIC Insurance

The Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation was created to protect bank deposits during the Great Depression.

1990s-Present

Digital Revolution

Online banking, mobile apps, and fintech innovations transformed how people access banking services.
